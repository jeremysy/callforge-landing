Living in a Parallel Universe: Why Your Business Runs on Outdated Assumptions

By Sylvan | January 2026

Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic, recently described what it's like to work in AI as "living in a parallel universe" - where you see the future unfolding while everyone else is still operating under old assumptions. I felt that.

Because I see it every day. Not in AI research labs, but in businesses. SMBs running on workflows that made sense in 2010. Companies where "that's how we've always done it" is the most dangerous phrase in the building.

The Parallel Universe Problem

Here's what Amodei meant: when you're deep in the weeds of what's possible with AI, you start seeing the world differently. Tasks that everyone else thinks are "just part of the job" suddenly look absurd.

And that's exactly what happened to me.

After building automation systems for businesses, I can't unsee it. When someone tells me their team spends 3 hours a day copying data from emails to Salesforce, I don't see "normal business operations." I see a parallel universe where that workflow was already automated 5 years ago.

Three Parallel Universes You Might Be Living In

Universe 1: The Manual Data Entry Universe

Your universe: Sarah spends 2 hours every morning copying lead information from inquiry forms into your CRM. "It's her job."

The parallel universe: That data flows directly into your CRM. Sarah spends those 2 hours actually talking to leads. Your close rate doubles.

The gap between these universes? About 4 hours of setup time and $0 in software costs.

Universe 2: The "We'll Listen to Calls Eventually" Universe

Your universe: You record every sales call and customer support interaction. They sit in a folder. Nobody has time to listen. You miss patterns until it's too late.

The parallel universe: Every call is transcribed, analyzed, and tagged automatically. Feature requests go straight to product. Frustrated customers are flagged before they churn. Your retention improves by 15%.

The gap? One workflow. One time.

Universe 3: The "Someone Has to Do It" Universe

Your universe: You hire someone specifically to handle repetitive administrative work. They're bored. You're paying $40K+ a year. Everyone knows it's inefficient, but "someone has to do it."

The parallel universe: That work runs automatically in the background. You hire someone who actually moves the business forward. Your team is engaged, not exhausted.

The gap? Usually one workflow that nobody questioned because "that's how it's done."

Why You Can't See the Parallel Universe

It's not your fault. When you're inside the system, the inefficiencies are invisible. They're normalized. Your competitors do it the same way. Your industry has always worked like this.

But here's the thing: your competitors are waking up.

The businesses that automate one workflow at a time - the ones that question every manual process - they're not working harder. They're living in a different universe. One where their teams have time to think. To strategize. To actually grow the business instead of maintaining it.

The Portal Between Universes

The shift isn't complicated. It doesn't require a massive transformation project or a consultant who bills in six figures. It requires one question:

"What work are we doing that shouldn't exist?"

Not "what could be more efficient." What shouldn't exist.

When Dario Amodei talks about parallel universes, he's talking about the gap between what's possible and what's normal. That gap exists in AI research. But it also exists in your business.

Every manual workflow that could run automatically. Every piece of data entry that could be eliminated. Every hour your team spends on busy work instead of actual work.

That's your parallel universe. And it's closer than you think.

What to Do Next

You don't need to automate everything. You need to automate one thing. Pick the most painful workflow. The one that makes your team groan. The one where you think "there has to be a better way."

There is. And once you see it work, you can't unsee it.

Welcome to the parallel universe. The one where your business doesn't run on copy-paste.
